Children are passed via the default `<slot />`.

## Mechanism

- Outer container has `overflow-hidden` to clip content at the viewport edges.
- A flex inner container uses the `animate-marquee` utility (defined in `global.css`) — `translateX(0)` to `translateX(-50%)` over 30 seconds.
- The `<slot />` is rendered **twice**. When the first set translates out of view, the duplicate has scrolled in to replace it — no visible seam.
- A "Trusted by leading data companies" label is absolutely positioned above the marquee with a `bg-canvas` background to punch through the border.

```astro
<LogoMarquee class="border-t border-stroke py-6">
  <img src="/logos/company-1.svg" alt="Company 1" class="h-6 w-auto shrink-0" />
  <img src="/logos/company-2.svg" alt="Company 2" class="h-6 w-auto shrink-0" />
  <img src="/logos/company-3.svg" alt="Company 3" class="h-6 w-auto shrink-0" />
</LogoMarquee>
```

## Gotchas

- You need enough children to fill the container width — otherwise the seam between the two duplicate sets is visible as a gap.
- The slot is duplicated in HTML, so each child appears in the DOM twice. Avoid putting interactive elements inside (clickable logos will have two activations registering against analytics, etc.).
- The animation is pure CSS. Speed comes from the `marquee` keyframes in `global.css` (currently 30s linear).
- The "Trusted by" label assumes the page background matches `--color-canvas` so its `bg-canvas` punches through any overlapping border cleanly.
